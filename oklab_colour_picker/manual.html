OKLab Colour Selector

A perceptual colour picker for Krita built on the OKLab colour space. Pick by lightness, chroma and hue and get perceptually equal selection both with sliders and on the picker.

Why OKLCH?

Regular HSV/HSL are fundamentally flawed sRGB representation, not perceptionally even. The same slider distance can result in a tiny nudge in perception in one place and a jump in another, and lightness shifts when you only meant to change hue.

OKLab was designed around how we actually see colour. In practice that means:

  • Hue changes keep the same perceived lightness.
  • Lightness changes don't shift hue.
  • Equal slider steps feel like equal visual steps.

Good for building palettes, shading, and matching tones without fighting the picker. Background: A perceptual colour space for image processing by Björn Ottosson (2020).

Tabs

Each tab fixes one axis and lets you pick the other two on a 2D slice:

  • Hue / Chroma — at a fixed lightness.
  • Hue / Lightness — at a fixed chroma.
  • Lightness / Chroma — at a fixed hue.

The fixed axis is the one you control with the matching L, C, or H slider underneath.

Picking

  • Drag to preview, release to commit to the foreground colour.
  • Use the L, C, H sliders or number boxes for precise values.
  • Type a hex value into the swatch (with or without #).
  • The arrow on the swatch reverts to the previous colour.

Out of Gamut

Since color slices are not equal, sometimes there are no equivalent colors when moving on different axes. If your pick sits outside the gamut, the swatch shows ⚠ and the committed colour is clipped to the closest in-gamut neighbour.
